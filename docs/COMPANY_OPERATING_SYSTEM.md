## What Stays Out Of Repo

- bank statements
- tax records
- incorporation paperwork
- raw legal contracts unless deliberately redacted and approved
- passwords, API secrets, and recovery codes
- HR or private personal records
- raw customer PII exports

## Company Goal

Operate one coherent product company where a customer can:

1. discover RinaWarp Terminal Pro
2. understand the value quickly
3. buy or start a trial
4. install and activate successfully
5. get support without guessing
6. stay retained because the product keeps delivering value

## Weekly Cadence

### Monday: Product and Launch Review

- review open blockers
- review release readiness
- review active product priorities
- choose the single most important launch or retention task for the week

### Wednesday: Funnel and Customer Review

- review traffic, trials, activation, conversion, churn, and support volume
- review the top customer-reported pain points
- decide whether messaging, onboarding, or pricing needs adjustment

### Friday: Ship and Retrospective

- ship the week’s highest-priority work
- update changelog or release notes if user-visible
- document what moved metrics or removed risk
- choose what carries forward into next week

## Operating Lanes

### Lane 1: Product

Owns:

- desktop product quality
- Companion quality
- trust, proof, recovery, and onboarding UX

Primary question:

- does the product actually deliver the promise cleanly?

### Lane 2: Revenue

Owns:

- pricing clarity
- checkout
- entitlement activation
- restore purchase
- billing support

Primary question:

- can a new customer become a paid, activated user without manual rescue?

### Lane 3: Support

Owns:

- issue intake
- launch bug triage
- billing and access recovery
- customer communication

Primary question:

- can a confused customer get unstuck quickly?

### Lane 4: Growth

Owns:

- landing page clarity
- screenshots and demos
- launch posts
- attribution and funnel measurement

Primary question:

- can we repeatedly get the right people to try the product?

## Decision Rules

- Do not prioritize internal architecture work over broken revenue, launch, install, billing, or support flows.
- Do not launch new messaging that the app cannot support honestly.
- Do not expand tiers or offers until the base Pro funnel is clear and measurable.
- Prefer one clear product story over multiple weak ones.

## Current Recommended Focus Order

1. release and installer reliability
2. billing and entitlement reliability
3. first-run activation quality
4. support responsiveness
5. conversion clarity
6. repeatable acquisition

## Exit Criteria For “Operating Company”

RinaWarp Technologies, LLC is acting like a fully operating company when all of these are true:

- release cadence is real
- support path is real
- billing and restore flows are real
- funnel metrics are reviewed weekly
- launch blockers are tracked openly
- product claims match shipped behavior
- one person being unavailable does not stop releases or customer recovery
