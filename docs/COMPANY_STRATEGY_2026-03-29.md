## Current Reality

As of `2026-03-29`:

- `RinaWarp Terminal Pro 1.1.11` is live
- the public website is `https://rinawarptech.com`
- pricing, checkout, restore, billing, and download surfaces are real
- production smoke and audit checks are real
- release/update metadata is real
- support and revenue operating docs exist

That means the company no longer needs a strategy centered on "make the product launchable."

The company now needs a strategy centered on:

1. getting more of the right people to try it
2. helping them activate successfully
3. converting them to paid
4. retaining them
5. removing trust friction that blocks growth

## What Is Already Strong

### Product

- Terminal Pro has passed targeted unit, E2E, packaged, and release-style checks.
- The current Agent surface is cleaner, calmer, and more professional than the earlier card-heavy version.
- The core proof, recovery, and trust story is stronger and more differentiated than generic AI terminal positioning.

### Website

- Home, Pricing, Team, Download, Docs, Packs, Support, and Account are aligned more closely than before.
- Real screenshots, a demo asset, clearer pricing/support copy, and an explicit trust story are live.
- The canonical site and release surfaces match the current brand and release.

### Release and Operations

- The desktop release lane is guarded.
- Smoke, audit, KPI snapshot, and revenue-report commands exist and run.
- Support workflows and launch-review docs exist.

## What Is Still Actually Missing

### 1. Repeatable Growth

The biggest unfinished business area is not engineering. It is acquisition.

The company still needs one repeatable channel that runs every week, such as:

- founder-led developer content
- X/Twitter demos
- Show HN / community launch follow-up
- developer workflow videos
- direct outreach to teams who care about proof and trust

Until one acquisition channel is working repeatedly, the company is still early even if the product is strong.

### 2. Proof Of Conversion

The revenue machinery exists, but the company still needs proof from real behavior:

- where people drop between homepage and pricing
- where they drop between pricing and checkout
- where they fail between download and first trusted run
- which users convert and retain

The next product roadmap should come from those answers.

### 3. Support-Led Product Learning

Support is defined, but it now needs to become a learning system:

- categorize every install, billing, unlock, restore, and first-run issue
- identify repeated friction quickly
- convert recurring friction into shipped fixes

### 4. Platform Trust Upgrades

The clearest commercial trust gaps are:

1. Windows signing
2. macOS signing and notarization

These are now business-priority trust upgrades, not speculative platform work.

### 5. Business Operations Outside Git

Some of the remaining work is intentionally not in this repo:

- banking
- accounting
- tax handling
- CRM
- secure customer/admin operations
- legal records

These still matter for "real company" status, even though they should not live in source control.

## Current Strategic Priorities

This is the recommended priority order now.

### Priority 1: Revenue And Activation

Primary question:

- are the right users getting from website to trusted first value without rescue?

Primary work:

- watch live funnel metrics
- improve first-run activation
- improve pricing clarity where needed
- remove install and restore friction

### Priority 2: Support And Retention

Primary question:

- when users get confused, do we save them fast enough to keep them?

Primary work:

- run inbox workflow daily
- tag and summarize all support issues
- ship fixes for repeated onboarding/install/access pain

### Priority 3: Trust Upgrades

Primary question:

- what prevents buyers from trusting installation and updates?

Primary work:

- fund and implement Windows signing
- fund and implement macOS signing/notarization

### Priority 4: Growth Execution

Primary question:

- can we reliably bring in the next set of qualified users every week?

Primary work:

- choose one growth channel
- run it consistently
- improve site/demo content from real audience response

### Priority 5: Feature Expansion Only With Evidence

Primary question:

- is the next feature the best revenue or retention move, or just more scope?

Primary work:

- defer major surface expansion unless support or funnel data justifies it
- treat Companion parity as important, but not automatically more urgent than conversion or trust work

## What This Means For Companion

Companion should not be framed as "missing everything."

Current reality:

- Companion already has a chat scaffold
- Companion now has a test baseline
- Companion is no longer pure concept work

So the strategy is not "build chat from nothing."

The strategy is:

- close the most commercially meaningful gaps between Companion and Terminal Pro
- do that only after the main Terminal Pro revenue loop is clearly working

## 30-Day Focus

### Week 1

- run daily support and KPI reviews
- identify the top 3 real launch frictions
- tighten whichever part of install, restore, or activation is losing users

### Week 2

- choose one acquisition channel and run it deliberately
- improve the site/demo content from actual reactions
- review download-to-activation behavior

### Week 3

- ship one retention or activation improvement from real user evidence
- prepare the Windows signing purchase/setup path if revenue supports it

### Week 4

- review conversion and support trends
- decide whether the next best use of time is:
  - trust upgrade
  - onboarding improvement
  - pricing adjustment
  - Companion parity work

## Simple Strategic Rule

Do not treat "more features" as progress if:

- acquisition is not repeatable
- activation is unclear
- support pain is recurring
- trust friction is blocking installs

At this stage, RinaWarp wins by becoming easier to trust, easier to activate, and easier to recommend.

## Current Top Five Actions

1. Run support inbox and KPI review daily for a full launch week.
2. Build one visible backlog of install, restore, billing, and onboarding friction.
3. Choose one repeatable acquisition channel and run it consistently.
4. Improve the highest-friction activation step from real evidence.
5. Fund and implement Windows signing when revenue or support pain justifies it.

## Success Condition

RinaWarp is becoming a successful company when all of these are true at the same time:

- the website gets qualified traffic repeatedly
- users convert from interest to install to activation
- paid users can stay paid and recover access cleanly
- support issues are handled quickly and become product fixes
- releases remain trustworthy and routine
- the next roadmap decisions come from customer evidence, not guesswork
